Geometric mean theorem 

This theorem describes the relationship between altitude drawn on the hypotenuse from vertex of the right angle and the segments into which hypotenuse is divided by altitude.

\(BD^2=AD\times DC\)

 Proof 

We know, △ADB ~ △ABC

Therefore, \(\tfrac{AD}{AB}=\tfrac{AB}{AC} \)(corresponding sides of similar triangles)
AB2=AD×AC
........1

but , AB2=AD2+BD2

by pythagoras theorem

AD2+BD2 =AD×AC

BD2 =AD×AC -AD2

BD2=AD(AC-AD)

AC-AD=DC

BD2=AD×DC
